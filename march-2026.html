A plain-English summary of how your emails performed in March — what's working, what to watch, and what's coming in April.
The Big Picture
March was a strong broadcast month and a major step forward for the welcome series — even as list growth took a seasonal breather.
Three major campaigns went out in March — the newsletter, and two enrollment teasers for Fall 2026. The newsletter hit 1,053 families at a 46.2% open rate. Promo Teaser #2 matched that energy with a 49.8% open rate. The welcome series is the real standout this month: average click rates jumped from 2.78% in February to 11.32% in March — the rebuild is clearly working. List growth slowed to a net +4 (vs. +31 in February), but that's a normal off-season pattern. April events will pick things back up.

What We're Watching
Three things worth keeping an eye on.
1
Opt-out uptick — 15 unsubscribes in March vs. 9 in February
Not alarming on its own — these are likely families who finalized a school decision elsewhere and are cleaning their inboxes. But if it continues to rise through April, we'll look at cadence and segmentation to reduce friction.
2
List growth slowed to net +4 in March
Fewer events meant fewer new signups. This is seasonal, not structural. The April event calendar (two Discovery Walks, one Open House) will drive the next pulse of organic growth.
3
Promo Teaser #1 subject line / timing — something to learn from
35.6% is still above industry average but below Hiba's norm. Worth reviewing what was different about that send (send time, subject, segment) to apply to future enrollment campaign emails.
