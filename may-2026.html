Overview
The Big Picture

A quiet but meaningful uptick — and the highest-CTR full-list send of the year.

May brought a quiet but meaningful uptick — 34 new profiles, up from 22 in April and 24 in March. Still soft by historical standards, but the trend is moving in the right direction ahead of summer events.

The standout story this month was the Teacher Spotlight — "Meet the teachers behind Hiba Academy" — which delivered the highest click rate of any full-list send this year: 59.8% open rate and 4.5% click rate on 1,047 recipients. Content that puts real people and faces front and center continues to outperform everything else for this audience.

May also marked the debut of a new campaign type: Application Journey emails. Two sends — one targeted (65 recipients, 58.5% OR) and one broader (635 recipients, 60% OR) — were designed to give interested families a clear, low-friction path to starting their Ravenna application. Early results are strong. This format is worth developing further.

The Preschool Partners email ("Help Your Families Discover Hiba Academy Through Summer Events") was a new outreach format targeting preschool directors — 185 delivered, 51.4% OR. First send of this type. Worth watching response.

The popup continues to be unresolved — now three consecutive months with no fix from the ITC team. It remains a drag on organic list growth and needs better visibility from the dev side before June. This is flagged for the review call.

Automated Flow
Long-Term Nurture Flow — First Real Data
First Full Month ✓

The Long-Term Nurture Flow launched in late April. May is its first full month of operation.

What's Live
Track A1
Families interested in Fall 2026 who haven't attended an event. Goal: get them to a Discovery Walk or Open House.
Track A2
Families who attended an event but haven't filled out their Ravenna application. Goal: move them to complete their application.
Track B
Families interested in Fall 2027 or later. Goal: long-term relationship building until they're ready to act.
May Data (LTN Events Flow)

First LTN email: 23 delivered — 69.6% open rate — strong early signal.
Second LTN email: 18 delivered — 61.1% open rate.

Both sends showing above-average engagement for families in the mid-funnel. Volume is intentionally small — the flow only triggers for families who meet specific conditions. As more families move through the welcome series and hit the LTN entry criteria, volume will grow. Full track-by-track breakdown will be available in the June report.

The infrastructure is working. The funnel is now fully connected: Meta/organic acquisition → signup → welcome series → LTN → Ravenna application.

Michelle Guan
Michelle first encountered Hiba Academy through a Meta Instagram ad in July 2025. She registered for 6 events between July and December — Open Houses, Discovery Walks, Jackson Park Family Day. The attendedEvent flag in her profile shows false across all six. Whether she couldn't make it or the data didn't sync is something we're confirming with the Hiba team at our next review.

What's clear: she never stopped engaging with emails. She subscribed in November 2025, clicked through on her first email, and kept receiving campaigns through the winter and spring. No single email appears to have triggered her final decision — she went direct to the Hiba website and started her Ravenna application on May 27, 2026.

10 months from first Instagram touch to application start. The emails didn't convert her in one send — they kept Hiba present until she was ready.
Some families need 10 months. The email program was there every step of the way.
